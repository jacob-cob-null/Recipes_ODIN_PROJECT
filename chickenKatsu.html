Chicken Katsu
Crispy, golden perfection! This Japanese favorite features juicy chicken coated in crunchy panko, fried to a crisp, and served with a rich, savory katsu sauce. Simple, satisfying, and utterly delicious!

4 servings

20 minutes

List of ingredients:


  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/2 cups panko
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, butterflied and cut into 4 cutlets
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable or canola oil
  • 2 cups finely sliced cabbage
  • Cooked white rice, for serving
  • 4 lemon wedges
  • 1/2 cup ketchup
  • 3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce

Procedures:


  1. In a small bowl, stir together ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, and soy sauce; set aside. For the chicken, set a wire rack on a baking sheet or line a plate with a paper towel. Place flour, eggs, and panko into three separate shallow bowls. Mix 1 teaspoon salt and 1 teaspoon pepper in a small bowl, then sprinkle over both sides of the chicken cutlets. Dredge each cutlet in flour, dip in eggs, and coat with panko, pressing firmly to adhere. Transfer to a plate.

  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat until it reaches 350°F. Test readiness by sprinkling panko into the oil—if it bubbles vigorously, it's ready. Fry two cutlets at a time for about 3 minutes per side until golden brown. Transfer to the prepared rack or plate, season with salt, and let rest for 2 minutes. Repeat with the remaining cutlets. Slice into 1/2-inch strips, serve on a bed of cabbage with katsu sauce, rice, and a lemon wedge.
