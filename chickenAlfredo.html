Chicken Alfredo
Creamy, rich, and utterly comfortingâ€”this Chicken Alfredo combines tender chicken with a luscious, garlicky parmesan sauce over perfectly coated pasta. A classic indulgence in every bite!

4 servings

25 minutes

List of ingredients:


  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 12 ounces fettuccine
  • Olive oil, for tossing
  • 12 ounces boneless, skinless chicken breast (about 2)
  • 1 stick (8 tablespoons) unsalted butter
  • 2 cups heavy cream
  • 2 pinches freshly grated nutmeg
  • 1 1/2 cups freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano

Procedure:


  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine and cook according to package instructions until al dente, meaning tender but still slightly firm. Drain the pasta and toss it with a splash of olive oil to prevent sticking.

  2. Slice the chicken into thin 1/4-inch strips and season with salt and pepper. Heat a large skillet over medium heat, then melt two tablespoons of butter. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the chicken in a single layer. Cook without moving for 1 to 2 minutes until browned, then flip and cook for another 2 to 3 minutes until fully cooked. Transfer the chicken to a bowl and set aside.

  3. Reduce the heat to medium and melt the remaining butter in the skillet, scraping up any browned bits for extra flavor. Whisk in the heavy cream and nutmeg, bringing the sauce to a gentle simmer. Cook for 2 minutes, then lower the heat and whisk in the freshly grated Parmesan. Once the sauce is smooth and creamy, return the cooked chicken and pasta to the pan. Toss everything together until well coated.
